Unit 1: Locational Significance

Assam covers 78,438 sq. km. It shares international borders with Bhutan and Bangladesh.

Q: Why is Assam called the "Gateway to NE India"?

It connects the rest of India via the 22km Siliguri Corridor (Chicken's Neck) and provides the primary transit route for the other six NE states.

Unit 2: Physical Characteristics & Problems

The state is divided into: Brahmaputra Valley, Barak Valley, and the Hilly Region.

Climate: Tropical Monsoon Rainforest climate with high humidity.

Q: State two causes of annual floods in Assam.

1. Extremely high monsoon rainfall. 2. Siltation of the riverbed (aggradation) causing reduced water-carrying capacity.

Unit 3: Natural Resources

  • Oil: Digboi, Naharkatiya, Moran.
  • Coal: Makum, Ledo.
  • Limestone: Karbi Anglong.

Section C: Long Answer (10 Marks)

Discuss the impact of river bank erosion on the socio-economic life of the people of Assam.

1. Landlessness: Thousands of hectares of fertile land are lost annually.
2. Displacement: Creation of 'Erosion Induced IDPs' (Internally Displaced Persons).
3. Economic Loss: Loss of livestock and standing crops.
